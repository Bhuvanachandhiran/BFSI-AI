GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

1. Definition and Scope

The Grievance Redressal Regulatory Framework establishes the structured process for addressing customer complaints within the institution. The Grievance Redressal Regulatory Framework ensures fair treatment, timely resolution, and regulatory compliance.

2. Regulatory Framework

Under the Grievance Redressal Regulatory Framework, complaints must be recorded, tracked, and resolved within prescribed timelines. The framework mandates a multi-level escalation structure, including branch-level resolution, nodal officer review, and external supervisory escalation mechanisms.

3. Compliance Requirements

The Grievance Redressal Regulatory Framework requires documentation of complaint resolution timelines, root cause analysis, and corrective actions. Compliance requirements ensure adherence to consumer protection guidelines and supervisory expectations.

4. Reporting and Monitoring

Periodic supervisory reporting must disclose complaint volumes, resolution rates, and systemic issues identified. The Grievance Redressal Regulatory Framework emphasizes transparency and accountability.

5. Risk Management and Supervisory Oversight

The Grievance Redressal Regulatory Framework supports operational risk mitigation by identifying systemic weaknesses. Supervisory oversight verifies compliance with regulatory directives and internal policy guidelines.
